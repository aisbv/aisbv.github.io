The Tradition

A Samasti Bhikshavandhanam

It is the duty of every one of us in grahastashrama to support the activities of our beloved Periyavals — offering our pranams in the form of bikshavandanam.

This is a samasti (group) Bhikshavandhana offered by suvasinis — a tradition begun in Maha Periava's days, organized by a small group of pious, tradition-bound and pious suvasinis.

Since offering Bhiksha to Sanyasis during the holy period of Chaturmasya is a deeply spiritual and divine act, and it is not possible for every suvasini across India to do this individually, Maha Periava conceived the idea of Samasti Bhikshavandhana. Last year, while accepting our Bhikshavandhana, Sri Sri Periava blessed all the participating suvasinis and guided us to include even more suvasinis in the years to come. Beyond the pooja itself, this gathering gives all of us the opportunity to take part in socially relevant work undertaken by Sri Mattam.

Begun by Chellamma Mami in the 1990s with a modest budget of ₹100, the Bhikshavandhanam has grown, through the devotion of suvasinis worldwide, to a 2025 participants of about 300 suvasinis and a budget of about ₹15 lakhs.

Traditional Offerings

  • Rice, jaggery, dhal, oil, ghee, honey, badam, cashew, elaichi, fruits, vegetables, flowers
  • Vastram for Periyaval
  • Pattu pavadai stiched with silk thread for Sri Maha Tripurasundari and Silk Vastram for Sri Chandramouleswara
  • Vastram for niyama brahmanas
  • Saree, blouse, silver metti, gold mangal sutra and other mangala dravyangal for the suvasinis
  • Kumkum, manjal, sambrani, camphor, ghee, oil for the pooja
  • Kamakshi Devi abhishekam and silk saree for Ambal
  • Gold coins for Maha Periyava and Pudhu Periyava adhishtanam kainkaryakarthas
  • Silver theertha patrams for periiyava kainkaryakarthas
  • Gold coin ofeering to periyava after bikshai
  • Sandalwood log (about 1.5 kg)
  • Dakshinai
This Year's Celebrations

The Two Day Bhikshavandhanam Program

As per tradition, the Samasti Bhikshavandhanam celebrations unfold across two days specified by Periyava.

Day One · Monday, 01 Sep 2026

Sri Kamakshi Temple and Sri Matam

Kanchipuram
  1. Early morning (4:30 am) Abhishekam to Goddess Kamakshi
  2. 10 AM — Abhishekam, Vishesha Pooja and Bhikshavandhana to Sri Sri Sri Maha Periava and Sri Sri Sri Pudu Periava Adhistanam at Sri Mattam, Kanchipuram
  3. Honouring of Kainkarya Karthas at the Adhistanam by the Srikaryam of Sri Mattam
Day Two · Tuesday, 02 Sep 2026

Sri Maha Periyava Manimantapam

Orikai, Kanchipuram
  1. Bhikshavandhana to His Holiness by suvasinis
  2. By 9:00 AM — a large gathering of suvasinis to join the procession for Sankalpam
  3. Nallanku & Suvasini Pooja
  4. Niyama Brahmana Pooja
